Horse racing tips: Newcastle, Cartmel, Chester, Doncaster, Newmarket and Yarmouth - Templegate's bet

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Sunday, April 14, 2024

NEWCASTLE

6.20

LET me point you in the direction of Letmestopyouthere.

The four-year-old shaped with a huge amount of promise on his first start for trainer Michael Bell when finishing third at Salisbury last time.

That was his best run for a while and he has now slipped to a dangerous mark.

Nuns Walk has been banging on the door of late and should be on the premises once again.

Course and distance winner Katheefa is another who looks well-weighted on past efforts.

Guardia Sivizzera did too much too soon last time and is respected for an in-form yard.

MISSING LADY Wesley Ward still puzzled by Lady Aurelia's no show in King's Stand

6.55

It's a case of love at first sight with Instant Attraction.

Jedd O'Keeffe's eight-year-old has been short of his best in recent runs but is having his first start since a breathing op.

He is now on a very attractive mark and could be a cut above his rivals.

Party Fears Too carries a penalty for his Hamilton win but is clearly in good nick.

Course and distance winner Rey Loopy is forgiven his flop at Redcar having missed the break, and the lightly-raced Amplification is also worth close consideration in an open race.

7.30

If in doubt, have a look at Var.

Paul Midgley's Orvar has a rock-solid record on the all-weather having won two of his three starts.

He had excuses when down the field at Musselburgh last time having been caught out wide, and he remains a sprinter with potential, particularly on an artificial track.

Templegate's TV tips

6.55 Newcastle - Instant Attraction: 'Slipping down the weights and has had a wind op' (add to your betslip)

7.30 Newcastle - Orvar: 'Excuses last time and will appreciate return to all-weather' (add to your betslip)

8.05 Newcastle - Four White Socks: 'Won nicely last time and looks a smart prospect' (add to your betslip)

There are plenty of dangers, headed by the once high-class Eastern Impact who hinted at a return to form when second recently.

Atletico loves this kind of surface and rarely runs a bad race, and the same can be said of Evergate who wears a first-time visor.

8.05

Pull your socks up and get stuck into Four White Socks.

Luca Cumani's promising filly caught the eye when second on debut at Windsor and she opened her account in great style at Goodwood earlier this month. She looks one to keep on side.

LATEST IN HORSE RACING

Smart Call was a Grade 1 winner in South Africa and ran an encouraging race when third in a Group 2 at York in May.

William Haggas has a 38% strike-rate at Newcastle, so his Maid To Remember deserves serious respect after she ran a good fourth in a Listed race at Nottingham last time.
