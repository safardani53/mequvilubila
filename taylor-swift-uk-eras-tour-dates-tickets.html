Taylor Swift reveals first UK gigs in six years as Eras Tour dates and tickets are announced

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Monday, August 5, 2024

TAYLOR Swift has revealed her first UK gigs in six years after officially announcing the European leg of her Eras Tour.

The US superstar will perform nine dates in the UK next summer, as well as a further two shows in Dublin.

She will kick off the newly-announced leg of her Eras Tour with two shows at Edinburgh’s Murrayfield Stadium, followed by two nights at Liverpool’s Anfield Stadium and one show at Cardiff’s Principality Stadium.

Taylor, 33, has been touring her native America with the epic music showcase and now UK-based Swifties can get in on the action.

The Anti-Hero singer will then perform two concerts at Wembley Stadium at the end of June, before returning to the same venue for two more gigs in August.

Announcing the news this afternoon, along with further shows in Asia, Australia and Europe, Taylor, 33, wrote on Instagram: “EXCUSE ME HI I HAVE SOMETHING TO SAY.

Taylor's UK and Ireland 2024 dates

TAYLOR Swift today revealed her Eras tour will include the following UK and Ireland cities...

7 & 8 June: Murrayfield Stadium, Edinburgh

14 & 15 June: Anfield Stadium, Liverpool

18 June: Principality Stadium, Cardiff

21 & 22 June: Wembley Stadium, London

28 & 29 June: Aviva Stadium, Dublin

16 & 17 August: Wembley Stadium, London

“I can’t wait to see so many of you on The Eras Tour next year at these new international dates!”

In total, she is expected to perform to more than 1 million fans while in the UK and Ireland.

Fans can get tickets by registering on each city’s official ticket registration page on Ticketmaster before Thursday June 22 at 11.59pm in the UK, and the same time on Friday for Ireland.

They will then receive a unique code to give them first access to purchase tickets.

Taylor last toured the UK and Ireland in 2018 when she played six Reputation Tour shows at stadiums in London, Manchester and Dublin.

Earlier this month, Taylor teased her dates as she wrote on Twitter: "LOTS more international dates to come soon, promise!"

It came following the announcements for her Mexico, Argentina and Brazil shows.

During her current Eras tour, she has delighted crowds in areas including Chicago, Detroit and the city of Pittsburgh, in her home state of Pennsylvania.

Yet the focus has been shared between tracks from her new album Midnights and her recent split with rocker Matty Healy.

Taylor was seen becoming hugely emotional during one break-up song on tour.

The Sun confirmed Taylor's romance with The 1975 frontman, 34, in a world exclusive last month - before revealing the pair's plans to move in together.

Yet in a shock U-Turn the pair have split following their whirlwind romance.

A friend recently said Taylor is “single again” and she is soon taking a short break from her Eras Tour of the Americas.

Her emotions were clear when she performed her Fifty Shades track I Don't Wanna Live Forever in a teary rendition on-stage.

Taylor, who originally recorded the break-up track with Zayn Malik was seen bleary-eyed when she sang the lyrics "Wondering if I dodged a bullet, or just lost the love of my life?"

Fans at the Chicago gig said her voice cracked with emotion during the poignant line.

Just weeks ago The Sound singer Matty was seen placing an arm tenderly on her back as they left a New York recording studio.

Taylor's ex of six years Joe Alwyn was then said to be "devastated" by how the Shake It Off songstress' new romance had unfolded - yet she was quick to tell of her sheer "happiness."

During a previous gig date for her Eras tour in the US, Taylor spoke to fans during songs.

Ahead of track Question...? from new album Midnights, she said: "I’ve just never been this happy in my life in all aspects of my life…

"It’s not just the tour…my life finally feels like it makes sense.

"So I thought I’d play this song which brings me a lot of happy memories."

She was also said to have sent Matty a secret message during her third Eras gig in Nashville.

Her final Eras tour date was previously listed for November 26 in Brazil.

Taylor previously toured her Reputation tour back in 2018.

Back then she entertained crowds with six massive stadium gigs at the Etihad Stadium in Manchester, Croke Park in Dublin and London's Wembley Stadium.
