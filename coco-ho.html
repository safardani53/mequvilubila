Hawaiian Surfing Royalty, Coco Ho [2023 Update]

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Friday, April 26, 2024

Tagged as the royalty of Hawaiian professional surfing, Coco Ho is the daughter of Michael Ho, the pioneer of pro surfing and godfather of the North Shore, Oahu surf scene.

The reason why Coco’s surfing is amusing is because of her natural and graceful style—she never tries to force anything.

The Hawaiian girl has lived and learned from various events for the past ten years. She is a champion. The way she plays with the ocean waves is a perfect art, and she has surfaced through several challenging waves.

Among women’s surfing tours, she has been standing undefeated at the top of the podiums.

Not to mention, the talented surfer, Coco, is the youngest women competitor to participate in the world tour. 

In this article, we will dive into the surfer’s life, professional career, her ups and downs on the World Tour, her rich surfing family history, net worth, and other lesser-known facts.

Coco Ho: Early Life and Family

Coco was born in 1991 in Honolulu, Hawaii, USA. Her close friends and family have seen Coco’s impressive evolution in surfing while growing up.

She completed her high school at a private, hybrid online school, Elite Element Academy. But later, Coco shifted her focus entirely to her surfing career.

Coco comes from a family of professional long-line surfers. Her unmatchable performances have made her the official heiress of a Hawaiian surf legacy.

But, while her family influenced and inspired her, they never pushed Coco too hard.

Though Coco’s family inspired and influenced her to surf, they never pushed her too hard.

Instead, She naturally got interested in a surfing career as she lived on the world-famous North Shore, which has some of the most amazing waves on the whole planet.

Grown-up in the Royal Family of Surfers

Her father is Michael Ho, the Triple Crowns winner, and appeared in five Pipe Masters final events.

Michael had made his name in surfing by finishing No. 3 in the world and winning eight Duke finals.

Her uncle Derek was the World Champion of1993 and a four-time Triple Crown winner.

Furthermore, Coco’s mom Brian Ho is a homemaker. 

Mason Ho, Reef ambassador, is Coco Ho’s brother. He is also known as “The World’s Most Entertaining Surfer.”

Coco started surfing at the early age of seven, following in the footsteps of her brother and father.

Elder brother Mason convinced Coco to participate in the surfing lineup when she was just nine years old. Michael wanted a surf partner at that time, and he couldn’t find any best option rather than his sister.

While growing up, Coco also looked upon her older brother, and she always used to try hard to impress her brother. Thus she always tried her best in surfing from a young age.

Also, Mason didn’t take much effort to convince his sister to join him at amateur events around the States.

Coco Ho: Professional Career

At the age of eight, Coco was sponsored for a surfing event. But her official debut took place at the age of 17 for the ASP Women’s Championship.

After a fantastic amateur career, she got her first big break in the 2009 Championship Tour.

She even won the Rip Curl Search event in Portugal in the tour and earned the title of Rookie of the Year for coming in fourth place in the world.

Later in the 2013 Tour, she faced a rough start. Although she was racking up the best scores, somehow, she was missing out on the final events.

After facing a brutal loss at Snapper Rocks, Coco went to West OZ, one of her loved waves.

She also participated in her fourth Tour stop in New Zealand and secured second place in Courtney Conlogue.

In 2017 alone, the Hawaii-born Coco came first in three QS events in Portugal, Spain, and Japan, giving her nine entire her career.
