During his time at Alabama, Miller established himself as one of the top players in the nation.

He gained widespread recognition as a consensus five-star recruit, a designation reserved for the most highly regarded prospects in the country.

Early Life and Family

Brandon Miller was born in St. Louis, Missouri, on November 22, 2002. His parents are Darrell Miller and Angela Youlanda Miller.

His father played football at the University of Alabama and later professionally overseas, while his mother was a track runner and a high school star athlete.

He has two older siblings, Darrell Miller Jr. and Britany Miller, who played college basketball. Darrell played basketball at Iowa State and later professionally in Europe.

Likewise, Britany played basketball at Cane Ridge High School and was a junior forward at Cumberland University. She graduated from Cumberland in 2022 with a degree in Business Administration.

Brandon grew up in Antioch, Tennessee, and he also attended Cane Ridge High School just like his sister.

College Career

Brandon had an extraordinary college career. During his freshman season at Alabama, the player received multiple accolades, including being named SEC Player of the Year and SEC Rookie of the Year.

Likewise, Miller was also recognized as a first-team All-Conference player and a consensus second-team All-American. Further, he also set records for Alabama, including a freshman-record 41-point game.

However, Miller struggled during the NCAA Tournament, shooting just 19% from the field. Alabama was eliminated in the third round of the tournament by San Diego State, with Miller going 3-of-19 from the floor.

Despite struggling in the NCAA Tournament, the player decided to forgo his remaining college eligibility and enter the 2023 NBA draft.
