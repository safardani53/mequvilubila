Peter Kay's Dance For Life 2018 tickets are on sale: Dates, prices and venues for the charity dance-

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Thursday, June 27, 2024

AFTER the success of last year's Dance For Life, Peter Kay is bringing his fun-packed dance-a-thon back to arenas across England.

Here's everything you need to know about the dance extravaganza, including how to get your hands on tickets.

What is Dance For Life 2018 all about?

Peter Kay's Dance For Life parties are essentially three hour dance-a-thons hosted by the popular comedian.

The dynamic DJ will be behind the decks spinning all of your favourite disco and sing-a-long classics.

With profits from ticket sales and any sponsorship money raised going to Cancer Research UK, this is not just a jolly night out, it’s also for a good cause.

Peter Kay said: "I’d encourage people to get themselves sponsored via JustGiving.com, where a special Dance For Life team page has been set up.”

When does Dance For Life 2018 start?

The tour kicks off in late January, with two dates in Coventry before a couple of months off.

Then, in March, Peter will be bringing the music to Liverpool, with dates in Manchester towards the end of the month and the start of April.

Where is Dance For Life 2018?

Peter Kay is steering clear of the south of England, with events confined to Coventry, Liverpool and Manchester.

The dates for Dance for Life 2018 are:

  • 26 January 2018 – Ericsson Indoor Arena, Coventry
  • 27 January 2018 – Ericsson Indoor Arena, Coventry
  • 2 March 2018 – SPACE by Echo Arena, Liverpool
  • 3 March 2018 – SPACE by Echo Arena, Liverpool
  • 30 March 2018 – Manchester Central, Manchester
  • 31 March 2018 – Manchester Central, Manchester
  • 1 April 2018 – Manchester Central, Manchester (CANCELLED)

How do I get tickets to Dance for Life 2018?

Tickets were available on Ticketmaster from 9am on Friday 28 July.

The tickets will set you back £31.35, including the booking fee.

Unlucky for those wishing to get in on the action, the majority of tickets have now sold out.

It could be worth checking Twickets to find unwanted tickets without getting ripped off.
