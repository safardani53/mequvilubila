How to Pick the Right Wrapping Paper

Style

Similar to selecting Christmas ornaments, wrapping paper should suit your — or your recipient's — style. Think about what you’ll be using the gift wrap for — a birthday, specific holiday, or simply to stock for any occasion that may arise. If you’re on the hunt for something seasonal, a traditional motif — like Santa for Christmas — can work; on the other hand, if you want something to transcend occasions, try something more muted, like this classic plaid print.

It’s also worth considering your recipient, if possible. Are you looking for wrapping paper to cover kids’ gifts or for the adults in your life? If the former, you can go a little more playful; for the latter, you may want to search for something a little more sophisticated.

Size and Paper Weight

Wrapping paper generally comes in two different formats: rolls and sheets. Both come in a wide range of sizes, so take a peek at the dimensions before settling on any one design. If you have something large to wrap, you'll want to make sure the length of your wrapping paper can handle it.

In general, sheets tend to be smaller and, therefore, better for smaller objects and standard boxes. You can usually get a little more mileage from wrapping paper rolls, even if they aren’t jumbo rolls.

While it can be hard to find paper weight information, this number is a good indicator of wrapping paper quality. The higher the grams per square meter (GSM), the heavier, and therefore, the better the wrapping paper quality. In general, wrapping paper with an 80 to 120 GSM is a good, quality paper.

Gridlines

Some wrapping paper comes with gridlines printed on the back, like Rifle Paper Co.'s Santa Wrapping Roll. This can help you measure exactly how much paper to use, plus cut straighter lines. The end result: A more efficient use of your wrapping paper — ultimately a time and budget-saver.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • How can you tell if wrapping paper is good quality?

    Paper weight is a great indicator of quality. If you have ever tried to use a flimsy wrapping paper, it likely ripped as soon as you pulled it over the corner of a box. That paper probably had a low paper weight. This figure is measured in grams per square meter (GSM) — the higher the GSM, the better the paper quality. 

    While this information can be difficult to find, look for it — if it’s there, anything between 80 and 120 GSM is a good bet. If this information isn’t there, manufacturer and price tend to be pretty good indicators. For example, though Rifle Paper Co. and Papyrus typically charge a bit more for their papers, they tend to be more substantial and have more elevated designs.

  • What is a good wrapping paper for kids?

    When wrapping presents for kids, you can opt for a playful pattern or lean into something extra-festive, such as Santa-covered paper, if you celebrate Christmas.

  • How much does wrapping paper cost?

    Wrapping paper can cost anywhere from a few dollars a roll to $20 a roll. If you're buying wrapping paper on the lower end, expect low-quality paper that rips easily. You should be able to purchase heavy-weight, high-quality paper for around $10 per roll.
