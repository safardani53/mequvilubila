We paid 1,000 for pedigree mini English bulldog - but she wont stop growing &amp; now she is destro

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Monday, January 1, 0001

A WOMAN has told of her shock after spending a hefty £1,000 on a mini pedigree bulldog which now won’t stop growing - and it even destroyed her house.

The TikTok user said the breeder promised her adorable pup wouldn't get any heavier than 9kg.

Now at 22kg, the woman told how the dog has been turning her entire house upside down.

In a clip video captioned "scammed", the New Jersey dog-mum showed a clip of her little puppy looking up into the camera from a sofa.

The caption read: "Breeder: she’s a mini English bulldog, she won’t be bigger than 20lbs."

The shot of the innocent-looking black, brown and white pup could not prepare viewers for what was about to come next.

The video then cuts to a shocking video of the dog - now much larger - with a chunk of the wall between her jaws.

"50lbs later destroying my entire house," the caption read.

The content creator said she was scammed by the breeder - but didn't say how much she paid for the “pedigree” dog.

The breeds usually sell for around £1,000 to £4,000 - but the woman didn’t comment on exactly how much she spent on her mini English Bulldog.

Miniature English Bulldogs, also known as Teacup Bulldogs, usually weigh up to 18kg when fully grown.

This pup is a whopping 4kg over that weight and is still growing.

In other videos with her beloved dog, the woman said: "Watching you grow has been nothing short of amazing… you were not even touching 3lbs when I got you, and here you are today.

"The sweetest pup, my little Sagey."

A Brit also realised that her £2,000 purebred Wheaten terrier was something totally different when it started changing colour.

The fluffy black and brown puppy began turning white two years after the owner purchased it, and she suspected that it may have been dyed by the breeder.
