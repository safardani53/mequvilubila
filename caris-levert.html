Where was Caris LeVert born? – Early Life

Caris Coleman LeVert, known as Caris LeVert, was born on 25 August 1994 in Columbus, Ohio, USA. But later, his parents moved to Pickerington, Ohio, when he was in second grade.

Additionally, he was born to parents Darryl Wayne and Kim LeVert. He grew up with a younger brother, Darryl Marcus, who is just eleven months younger than him. 

Apart from this, his brother is also a basketball player who played for Connors State College.

Family

The interesting thing is, that he comes from a musical family as he is the third cousin of Eddie LeVert, the lead vocalist of the O’Jays.

Besides that, he is also the cousin and brother of late singer-songwriters Gerald LeVert and Sean LeVert, Eddie’s sons.

Moreover, his mother, Kim, is a Columbus City School’s first-grade teacher. His father, Darryl Wayne, was a graphic designer. But sadly, the LeVert family had to experience the tragic moment of Darryl Wayne’s death on April 4, 2010.

So, he had to lose his father at the age of 16. However, his mother raised both her sons very well.

Now, she must be the proudest mother because her sons are moving towards success in their fields.

In one interview, he remembered his dad and said, 

“I’m ok with it now, at peace, I guess. I feel like I got everything I needed to know from him. He taught me so much.”

While talking about him, LeVert also mentioned how calm and caring he was. 

“His big thing was keeping a level head – never get too high or too low. Just be calm, be smart.”

High School Highlights 

This 29-year-old basketball star started his high school career at Pickerington High School Central.

As a high school senior, he led Pickerington to a 26–2 record and the 2012 OHSAA Division I state championship.

Consequently, he became a 2012 Associated Press All-Ohio Second Team high school basketball player and the 2012 Columbus Dispatch Metro Player of the Year.

Furthermore, he was just a three-star recruited prospect in high school. But he committed to Alabama State University to play college basketball.

Meanwhile, he left Alabama State and enrolled at Michigan University on May 11, 2012.

College Stats

LeVert began his college career with Michigan Wolverines behind Stauskas, Robinson, Tim Hardaway Jr., and Matt Vogrich for small forward positions.

He had spent 4 years in Michigan, where he recorded his career-high points, rebound, and assists many times. In his college career, his contribution was impressive, and he led the team in winning several times.

The Highlighting part is he became the 4th player in Michigan’s history who made a triple-double record and the 49th Wolverine to eclipse 1,000 career points.

Additionally, the Wolverines star had gone through three surgical procedures to repair a stress fracture in his foot throughout the season.

Because of his second surgery, he had to miss the chance to select in the 2015 NBA Draft due to his foot’s reinjury.

However, he announced to return to the season game on April 21, 2015. Returning to the game again would allow LeVert to improve his pick-and-roll decision-making and defensive skills.

NBA Draft

Indeed, it was not easy for LeVert to travel until NBS’s road. However, he made it happen.

While facing foot injury many times, he finally got selected with the 20th overall pick in the 2016 NBA draft by the Indiana Pacers.

But later, the Brooklyn Nets acquired his rights and had signed a multi-year contract with him.

Before the 2016 NBA draft, LeVert wrote an open letter to NBA general managers and his doubters to ensure his capacity.

He wrote,

“I’m almost there. When I make my NBA debut, I can’t wait to show the world a player who can handle, shoot, go hard to the hoop, and defend.”

LeVert then stated how his versatility is his greatest asset. 

“I can play as a one, a two, or three. My versatility is my greatest asset, and I bring a unique skill set to the court.”

Career High Points and Injury 

Unstoppable rising star started his professional career with Brooklyn Nets. As his college career, he continued to make career-high points in his professional career.

Likewise, he made his professional debut against the Denver Nuggets, where he posted 4 rebounds and 3 steals in 9 minutes of play in the 2016 season.

Consequently, he became the first Net player who posted 3 steals in his debut.

During the first anniversary of his NBA debut, he scored career-high 21 points and 10 assists against reigning NBA MVP Russell Westbrook.

It led the Nets to a 100–95 win over the Oklahoma City Thunder in Mexico City in the 2017 season.

His performance was highlighted by his first NBA double-double on offense and also praised for his 2-steal/2-block/5-rebound effort on defense against Westbrook.

However, he missed 10 games of the 2017 season due to the minor groin and knee injuries.

Furthermore, he set a new career-high with 28 points in a 107–105 and won over the New York Knicks in the 2018 season.

On August 26, 2019, a 6 ft 6 in-205 pounds player had extended a three-year contract with the Nets. Likewise, he posted his career-high 29 points against the Phoenix Suns.

Likewise, LeVert recorded a career-high 51 points, including 37 in the fourth quarter and overtime, which led the Nets to a 129–120 win over the Boston Celtics in the 2019 season.

Caris was traded to Indiana Pacers for the 2021-2022 seasons. He recorded 13 points, seven rebounds, and two assists with a winning score of 122–111 against the Phoenix Suns.

After he was traded to Cleveland Cavaliers for the 2022 season, Caris scored a winning score of 105–92 against the San Antonio Spurs.
