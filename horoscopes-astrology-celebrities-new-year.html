I'm an astrology expert and here's what the horoscopes have in store for royals, celebs, sports star

Posted by Martina Birk on Wednesday, June 5, 2024

LIFE can be a rollercoaster of soaring highs and crushing lows for all of us – and especially the great, the good and the famous.

Some of the world’s top clairvoyants and astrologers have revealed what is written in the stars for our royals, politicians, sports personalities and celebrities in 2023...and it is set to get ugly for a few of them.

Which politician’s past will continue to haunt him despite his best efforts?

Which royal will face very own annus horribilis?

Who will smash a decades-old sporting record and which celebrity will find true love after a break-up that battered their confidence?

Below, a leading panel of astrologers reveal all . . .

ROYALTY

By Debbie Frank

KING CHARLES: This year looks challenging. This month’s publication of Prince Harry’s memoir Spare is bleak as Saturn squashes the King’s sensitive nature.

And the coronation date of May 6 is just hours after an eclipsed Full Moon, marking a turbulent reign.

From July the rebel planet Uranus, a radical force for change, means Charles will go through a time of challenges.

PRINCE WILLIAM: Neptune’s disappointing influence speaks of sadness over his flyaway brother.

However, in spring he comes out of his Cancerian shell into a radiant 2023. From the coronation onwards he’ll carve out a strong platform as Prince of Wales.

Autumn is huge for making a global impact.

PRINCE HARRY: Volatile Uranus arrives on his moon, a once-in-a-lifetime scenario which marks out a year of seismic change that will see a home move and a clear cut with the wider family.

The coronation, with its lunar eclipse on Harry’s Saturn and Midheaven, closes the book on his old life.

He will march forward into a life that is free and independent from the royals.

KATE, PRINCESS OF WALES: Kate’s year has the hand of destiny upon it, as if she’s been prepared for this moment to step out with new-found confidence.

Even though she is a reserved sun sign, Capricorn, we are going to see a different side of her – more accessible and spontaneous. June is her month to captivate the hearts and minds of the public.

DUCHESS OF SUSSEX: Meghan is gung-ho and in ­jubilant spirits in January.

She’s centre stage in March as Jupiter levels up her sense of success and 2023 puts her in warrior queen mode.

There’s a home move to another property or location coming, and the mid-October eclipse on Pluto is a wake-up call around family.

PRINCE ANDREW: As Saturn moves on to Andrew’s Sun in Pisces for the first time in 29 years, he’s facing a time that is stamped with karma – the consequences of ­previous actions.

Coupled with this, Pluto – the planet of power and disempowerment – is on his Mars and Venus, meaning Andrew will experience circumstances beyond his control. This may seem to him like betrayal.

SPORT

By Demian Allen

ERLING HAALAND: He will break Alan Shearer and Andy Cole’s record of 34 goals in a Premier League season.

But he has a bit of a problem coming: Pluto has been opposite his sun in Cancer, so he has drawn power off that. But in March, Pluto leaves Capricorn and he loses status or power.

So, until May he needs to be careful of injuries.

JUDE BELLINGHAM: Jude had his sun in Cancer with Mars in Pisces at the time of his birth, which means he is very emotional.

He is someone who plays using his feelings so he needs a manager who understands that.

In terms of a transfer I think Liverpool will be his next destination.

Real Madrid will try to get him, but I feel because of his sun in ­Cancer he is a homebody at heart.

JACK GREALISH: Jack is a Virgo with Mars in Scorpio and this means he’s not firing on all cylinders – but his form will bounce back from March to May.

However, he has a Saturn return coming up, which tends to be a difficult time.

This means Jack may experience relationship turbulence in the second half of 2023 and his football may suffer.

BEN STOKES: A year of steady progress beckons. He’s revolutionising cricket and as a Gemini he is being supported by the planets in 2023, although not in 2024.

I don’t think Ben will quite lead the team to the heights of 2022 but he will build firm foundations for the future.

WOMEN’S WORLD CUP: When the World Cup takes place this year, Venus is going backwards in the sky, known as retrograde.

This is going to cause problems and, sadly for the ladies, it means it will be one step forward and two steps back. I don’t see them replicating their Euros success.

CELEBS

By Jayne Wallace

ED SHEERAN: I see Ed holding a baby son. Towards the end of the year he will have news surrounding an addition to his family.

In 2023 he’s going to be the happiest he’s ever been and he will continue to enjoy ­support and love from family and friends.

By the end of next year he will step away temporarily from the public eye.

STORMZY: The past is the past when it comes to love, and I don’t see a rekindling with Maya Jama.

Instead, I see Stormzy becoming more in touch with his spirituality.

Career-wise he’s got a great year ahead. I see him working with some powerful ­people, and some kind of collab with Adele.

DUA LIPA: She may have had panic and stress in her life and phe last year was tough for her. But this year she’s on the right path.

I see her family giving her a lot of strength. Anything she does around design and ­creativity will be amazing.

I see a lot of work around branding and marketing. I only get positive vibrations.

HELEN SKELTON: Will continue to grow in self-belief and confidence.

She will become open to new love and new beginnings around August.

The new guy I see later this year is a “keeper” and there will be talk of weddings in 2024 and a little girl.

Within the BBC there will be numerous show offers.

BRITNEY SPEARS She will have a new lease of life, it’s like she has gone back to being 21.

I see her being very “together”, in control. I feel that Elton John has helped her incredibly with everything.

I think she’s going to get out there and announce a world tour.

We are also going to see a fly-on-the-wall show about her new life. I see the fulfilment of dreams and happiness.

POLITICS

By Babs Kirby

RISHI SUNAK: The stars are against him in the face of the problems the country is ­having.

Will he survive much beyond June, when Uranus contacts his Taurus sun, ­indicating yet more turbulence? I doubt it.

Uranus transits are unpredictable and it’s often a time when the truth comes out.

And with Uranus contacting his sun from June until October, it won’t be an easy time at all.

SIR KEIR STARMER: Keir is a slow burner and in his natal chart he has a sun-Pluto conjunction which indicates someone who wants power.

His progressed sun is in Scorpio, which is also a sign of being interested in power.

When combined with his links to Mars, he will become a force to be ­reckoned with in the coming 12 months and won’t make any wrong moves.

BORIS JOHNSON: Bojo has buckets of Venusian charm and the scandals and ­difficult questions won’t catch up with him just yet.

In the short term I can’t see him seeking the burden of power when he can earn shedloads as an ex-PM.

Another run for the top job is not in his self-interest – and astrologically, that comes first.

MATT HANCOCK: His stars say that while he’ll try to make a fresh start, his past will plague him through the year.

Progressed Mercury is sitting right on his natal Neptune, which signifies scandal. In addition, his need to be liked will be his downfall.

He’s got four planets in Libra and that’s often a sign of someone who is a people-pleaser.

JOE BIDEN: The stars indicate he probably won’t run for a second term. He will be feeling his age and slowing down.

It’s time to step aside and take it easy. But he’ll be remembered fondly, as he has a sun and Venus conjunction, very like Boris.
