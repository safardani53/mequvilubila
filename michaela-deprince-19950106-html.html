Michaela Mabinty DePrince is a Sierra Leonean American ballet dancer. Along with her adoptive mother, Elaine DePrince, Michaela wrote the book Flying Airplane: From a war orphan to star ballerina. She gained popularity after starring in the 2011 documentary First Position, following her and other young ballet dancers as they prepare to compete at the Youth America Grand Prix. She previously danced with the Dance Theater in Harlem as the youngest dancer in company history and is currently dancing as a soloist for the National Ballet of the Netherlands. Since 2016, Michaela has been a goodwill ambassador for the Dutch organization War Child, based in Amsterdam.
