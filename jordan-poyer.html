Jordan Poyer | Professional Career

Philadelphia Eagles

Jordan Poyer landed in the NFL world in 2013, selected by the Philadelphia Eagles in the seventh round of the 2013 NFL Draft.

On May 9, 2013, Poyer agreed on a four-year $2.2 million contract, including a signing bonus of $60,256.

Similarly, he played as a backup cornerback against Brandon Boykins, Brandon Hughes, Curtis Marsh Jr., and Trevard Lindley during the training camp.

Jordan Poyer made his first professional debut against the Washington Redskins and had a 33-27 victory over the team.

The same year, he made his first career tackle playing with teammates Nate Allen and Fred Davis.

Cleveland Browns

While playing with the Cleveland Browns, he recorded season-high six solo tackles during a 27-26 loss against the NewEngland Patriots.

On December 14, 2014, he had season-high four combined tackles in the Browns’ 30–0 loss to the Cincinnati Bengals.

Later on, he had a season-high three solo tackles in a 20-0 loss against the Baltimore Ravens.

Moreover, Poyer had a promising career in 2015. Jordan recorded three solo tackles as the Browns lost 26–23 to the Denver Broncos.

In the 14th week, he recorded two solo tackles and his first career sack against the Blaine Gabert with a 24-10 victory over the San Fransico 49ers.

He accomplished the 2015 season with 43 combined tackles, four pass deflections, two interceptions, and a sack in 14 games.

Poyer started his 2016 season playing in a loss against the Philadelphia Eagles.

On September 25, 2016, he had career combined tackles of 13 and deflected a pass during a 30–24 loss against the Miami Dolphins.

In 2016, he accomplished 39 combined tackles and two deflections in six games and six starts.

He accomplished the 2017 season with 94 combined tackles, 13 pass deflections, five interceptions, two sacks, and a touchdown.

His 2018 season was finished with 100 combined tackles, four interceptions, and two sacks.
