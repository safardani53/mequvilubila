Did Nick Eardley leave the BBC?

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Thursday, February 1, 2024

THE BBC is known for it's first class political reporting.

At the frontline of these stories is political correspondent Nick Eardley - but some fans have questioned whether he is leaving the Beeb.

Has Nick Eardley left the BBC?

Nick Eardley is known for being a top political reporter for the BBC..

However, some fans took to X (formerly known as Twitter) to question whether he is leaving his job.

This one tweeted: "Nick Eardley is the current BBC Political correspondent..not sure if he's leaving though."

While another simply said: "Is Nick Eardley leaving the BBC?"

Read more on BBC news

But viewers need not worry as Nick is not leaving his post at the BBC.

A spokesperson for the BBC told The Sun: "Nick has no plans to leave the BBC. He’s a core part of the Politics team."

Nick also continues to be active on his X account, reposting many BBC stories, and commenting on topical political events.

Commenting on PMQs on September 13, 2023, he tweeted: "Rishi Sunak says Govt is committed to triple lock at PMQs.

"But no firm commitment over whether commitment will be in the manifesto for next election."

Who is Nick Eardley?

Nick Eardley is a political correspondent for the BBC, after working his way up the ladder at the broadcaster.

Born in Edinburgh, he kicked off his career when did degrees in both politics and journalism.

Fresh from uni, Nick got his first taste of being a journalist when he worked for The Scotsman newspaper.

However, he then moved to London to join the BBC in 2014, initially part of their online team.

But by 2015, Nick had been appointed BBC Scotland's Westminster Correspondent, before getting a huge promotion in 2022 to become the BBC's main political correspondent.

Away from work and the TV star is a keen hiker and also enjoys running.

In fact in April 2023, he ran the London Marathon, telling his followers on Instagram: "What a day!!! Legs weren’t quite up to sub-3. Cramp featured heavily in the last 45 mins.
