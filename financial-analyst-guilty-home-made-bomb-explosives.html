Financial analyst found guilty of making home-made bomb and possessing explosives

Posted by Martina Birk on Saturday, June 29, 2024

A FINANCIAL analyst was yesterday found guilty of making a home-made bomb and possessing explosives.

Asad Bhatti, 49, was reported to police by the owner of a computer repair shop who became suspicious of content on his laptop.

Experts found a 173-page document compiled by Bhatti including a “simple guide to explosives” and sections on handguns and sniper rifles, Islam, Jihad and martyrdom.

A search of his home and a storage unit uncovered “chemicals and circuitry” for the construction of explosive devices, an improvised explosive device and an improvised explosive known as black powder.

Bhatti claimed that he had gathered the materials for lawful experimentation and out of curiosity.

He denied two counts of possessing explosives, one of making explosives and two of possessing articles for terrorist purposes.

His first Old Bailey trial was halted when he contracted Covid and fell ill.

A jury in his retrial deliberated for a week before finding him guilty on all charges.

Bhatti, of Redhill, Surrey, was remanded in custody and will be sentenced on April 25.
