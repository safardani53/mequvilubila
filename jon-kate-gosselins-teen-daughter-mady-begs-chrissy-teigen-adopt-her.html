Jon and Kate Gosselins teen daughter Mady begs Chrissy Teigen to adopt her as divorced pare

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Monday, September 9, 2024

JON and Kate Gosselin's daughter Mady has called on Chrissy Teigen to "adopt" her in the midst of her parents' ongoing bitter feud.

Chrissy shared a throwback picture of her at a 2011 Ed Hardy fashion show, and captioned it with the claims that Jon, who then starred on TLC reality show Jon and Kate Plus Eight, was sat in the front row and she was left "star struck."

In reply, Mady joked: "ADOPT ME! & become my second mom. Pls and thank you, Chrissy."

Mady is Jon and Kate's eldest daughter, along with her twin sister, Cara.

Chrissy commented on the throwback pic: "2011. Ed Hardy fashion show. Almost positive Jon Gosselin sat front row. I was star struck...."

The reality star and divorced father of eight replied in the comments: "...star struck? Random post but thank you and I hope you and John Legend are safe and healthy."

Jon currently lives with two of his youngest sextuplets, Colin and Hannah, while the other four live with his ex-wife, Kate.

Jon and Kate have been embroiled in a vicious court battle for custody since their 2009 divorce.

Their sextuplets, who all starred in TLC's Jon and Kate Plus Eight, are now allowed to decide which parent they wish to live with as they have turned 16.

Jon, who has previously claimed Kate "mentally tortured" their sextuplets and secretly had son Collin admitted to an institution – claims she has denied – previously said he's finally at peace with the situation and will support his children no matter what they decide.

"Leah, Aaden, Joel and Alexis, I love you. You're welcome to come over, call Hannah, Collin," he said.

Jon has called on the children to "go meet each other," hoping that when the 16-year-olds finally get their driving licenses, they will pick each other up for school and hang out.

He said: "My wish is that all my kids would just be together, like, hang out together.

"Kate and I don't live that far away from each other and I feel like there's an invisible wall.
