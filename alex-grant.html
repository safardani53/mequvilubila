Career

  • Grant played four seasons of major junior hockey with Saint John and the Shawinigan Cataractes in the Quebec Major Junior Hockey League after being picked by Saint John in the first round (first overall) in the 2005 QMJHL Entry Draft.
  • On June 24, 2013, the Pittsburgh Penguins exchanged Grant for winger Harry Zolnierczyk in a deal with the Anaheim Ducks.
  • He was re-signed to a one-year deal on July 10, 2013.
  • In his first NHL game, Grant’s first goal came against San Jose Sharks goalie Antti Niemi on November 30, 2013. On December 12, 2013, he was sent to the Norfolk Admirals of the American Hockey League after scoring in his second straight game with the Ducks. As the trade deadline drew near, the Ducks sent right-winger Andre Petersson to the Ottawa Senators in exchange for Grant on March 5, 2014.
  • On July 2, 2015, Grant agreed to a one-year, two-way deal with the Arizona Coyotes.
  • On July 4, 2016, Grant agreed to a one-year, two-way deal with the Boston Bruins. He played with the Providence Bruins of the American Hockey League for the entire 2016–17 season, when he led the league with 49 points in 70 games and was second in goals with 17.
  • On July 1, 2017, Grant left the Bruins and signed his third one-year, two-way deal with the Minnesota Wild. For the 2017–18 season, Grant was sent to the Iowa Wild, the Wild’s AHL affiliate. In 73 games, he recorded 13 goals and 36 points.
  • Grant signed a one-year deal with Jokerit of the Finnish Kontinental Hockey League on June 15, 2018.
  • In January 2022, Grant was selected to represent Canada in the Winter Olympics.
