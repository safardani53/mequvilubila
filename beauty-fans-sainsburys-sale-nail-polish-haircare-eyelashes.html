Beauty fans scramble to Sainsburys as nail polish, haircare, eyelashes and more are reduced to le

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Sunday, May 19, 2024

BEAUTY fans are rushing to their local Sainsbury's to stock up on make-up and haircare buys in the sale.

We all love a bargain, and the post-Christmas sales are the best time to stock up.

So if you're in the mood to stock up your make-up bag or toiletries, then you better get down to Sainsbury's quickly.

One bargain hunter took to social media to share the massive haul of beauty buys she bagged in the sale.

Taking to the Facebook group, Extreme Couponing and Bargains UK group, which boasts over 2million members, Amy Jane shared her finds.

"Sainsbury's bargains today," she captioned the post along with a picture of her goodies.

The savvy beauty shopper managed to grab three hair clips for just 88p each, two Phil Smith hairbrushes for £1.25 each and a pack of hairbands for £1.50.

She also managed to stock up on lots of nail essentials such as the Sally Hansen No Chip top coat for just 60p a bottle - a deal she decided to stock up on and buy three of.

Amy also grabbed two bottles of Sally Hansen Nail Growth primer for £1.10 each as well as a bottle of Complete Care nail treatment for just £1.10 as well.

The lucky shopper also picked up two gel nail polish kits from the brand Sensationail for just £2.50 which usually retail for £22 each.

Finally, Amy stocked up on a whole stash of fake eyelashes from Eyelure for just 50p a set and Kiss 3D lashes for 80p a set.

Her massive beauty haul ended up costing her just £22.35 and will keep her stocked up for months.

People were stunned by Amy's bargain haul and took to the comments to congratulate her on her bargains and vowed to check out their local supermarket.

One person wrote: "I'm starting to love that supermarket."

Another commented: "I am so jealous, I don't seem to find any bargains."

"We need to go to Sainsbury's tomorrow," penned a third.

Meanwhile a fourth asked: "were these on shelf or clearance rack?"

"On normal shelf but the label was reduced," replied Amy.
